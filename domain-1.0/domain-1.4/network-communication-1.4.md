
<p><h1>Network Transmission</h1></p>

<p><h1>Unicast</h1></p>

<p>One station sending information to another station (one-one), this does not scale well. It is fine for web surfing and file transfers though.</p>

<p><h1>Multicast</h1></p>

<p>Delivery of information to interested systems (one-to-many), difficult to scale across large networks. Multimedia delivery and stock exchange information are great examples.</p>

<p><h1>Anycast</h1></p>

<p>Single destination has multiple paths to two or more endpoints. Anycast packets are delivered to the closest interface.</p>

<p><h1>Broadcast</h1></p>

<p>Send information to everyone at once (One-to-all) in a LAN, routing updates are ARP requests are frequently done here only in IPv4.</p>
