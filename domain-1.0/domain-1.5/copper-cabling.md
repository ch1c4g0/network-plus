

<p><h1>Twister pair copper cabling</h1></p>

<p><h2>Balanced pair operation,</h2></p>

<p>Two wires with equal and opposite signals<br>
may look like Transmit+, Transmit- / Receive+, Receive-</p>

<p>The twist of the cable is the secret, it keeps a single wire constantly moving away from the interface. Opposite signals are compared at the end.</p>

<p><h1>Cable Transmission</h1></p>

<p><h1>Cable Speed</h1></p>

<p>Cables dont have a speed, they are just the median for electrical signals being sent over the wire. The encoding of that data will determine how much data can be transmitted over the wire.</p>

<p><h1>Cable Standards</h1></p>

<p>Cable standards are described as "Categories" i.e Category 5, Category 6.</p>

<p><h1>Coaxial Cables</h1></p>

<p>Often used in television/digital cable as well as high-speed internet.</p>

><p><h3>Common Uses</h3></p>
>
> - RG-6, Cable TV and Satelite Internet
> - RG-59, commonly used for CCTV and Security Cameras.
> - RG-11, Better performance over long distances, better attenuation.

<p>Twinaxial cables(two-inner conductors), common on 10 Gigabit Ethernet, SPF+ Cables.</p>

<p>Plenum space, space between drop ceiling and the ceiling.</p>

<p>Important to use plenum rated cable, plenum-rated cable use a PVC jacket, not as flexible as regular cable.</p>
