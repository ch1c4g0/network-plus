

<p><h1>RJ11 Connectors</h1></p>

<p>Registered Jack type 11, 6 position, 2 conductors (6P2C)<br>
Commonly used for Telephone and DSL connections</p>

<p><h3>Digital Subscriber Line (DSL)</h3></p>

<p>A broadband internet service that transmits data over existing copper telephone lines,<b>
allowing for simultaneous internet use and phone calls. Ranges from 1 to 100Mbps.</p>

<p><h1>RJ45 Connectors</h1></p>

<p>Registered Jack type 45, 8 position, 8 conductors (8P8C), its a modular ethernet connector.</p>

<p><h1>F-connector</h1></p>

<p>Coaxial cable, standard connector type, threaded connector.<br>
Commonly used with Cable modems from the Cable Television Infrastructure.<Br>
DOCSIS(Data Over Cable Service Interface Specification)</p>

<p><h1>BNC Connector</h1></p>

<p>Bayonet Neill-Concelman connector.</p>

<p>A way of locking coax or twinax cables.</p>
