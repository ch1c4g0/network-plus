

<p><h1>What is a Transceiver?</h1></p>

<p>A transmitter and receiver usually in a single component.</p>

<p>It provides a modular interface allowing you to add the transceiver that matches your network.</p>

<P><H1>Different Types</H1></P>

> - Ethernet
> - Fibre Channel
> - NOT compatible with each other

<p><h1>SFP and SFP+</h1></p>

<p>Small Form Factor Pluggable(SFP)</p>

<p>Most commonly used for 1 Gbit/s Fiber 1 Gigabit RJ45 also available</p>

<p><h1>Enhanced Small Form-Factor Pluggable(SFP+)</h1></p>

<p>Same physical size a SFPs, can support data rates up to 16 Gbit/s, common with 10 Gigabit Ethernet.</p>

<p><h1>QSPP</h1></p>

<p>Quad Small Form-Factor Pluggable</p>

<p>4-channels of SFP = Four 1 Gbit/s = 4 Gbit/s</p>
<p>Same thing for SFP+ Four 10 Gbit/sec = 40 Gbit/sec</p>

<p>It just combines four SFP's into a single transceiver providing cost savings on fiber and equipment.</p>
