

<p><H1>Fiber Communication</H1></p>

<p>Transmission by light, does not emit RF making it secure and difficult to tap, also has a long length emission</p>

<p>Fiber lines have a protective a light source, typically and LED or Laser, a thick protective coating called a buffer coating, a Low Reflective Core called Cladding, and a High Reflective Core to bounce the light around.</p>

><p><h1>Two different types,</h1></p>
>
> - Multimode Fiber, short-range communication(2km), inexpensive light source(LED).
> - Singlemode Fiber, Long-range communication(100km), Expensive light source(Lasers)

<p>Multimode cores are often larger larger than the wave length of the light allowing it to use different modes to transmit through the fiber.</p>

<p>Singlemode cores are much smaller allowing only one mode, this allows it to travel a longer distance.</p>
