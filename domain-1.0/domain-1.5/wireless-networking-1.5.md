

<p><h1>Wireless Standards</h1></p>

<p>Wireless Networking (802.11) is managed by the IEE LAN/MAN Standards Committee(IEEE 802).</p>

***<p>IEE is Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers</p>***

<p><h1>Interoperability</h1></p>

<p>Interoperability is handled by the WiFi-Alliance, with the standards being set by IEEE.</p>

**<p>Modern examples: 802.11ax or WiFi-6</p>**

<p><h1>IEE 802.11 Standard Evolution</h1></p>

**<p>802.11a-g are outdated and not used anymore, you may still see Wi-Fi 4.</p>**

**<p>802.11ac,ax,be are most commonly used today.</p>**

<p>One of the biggest changes overtime is the link-rate, it is constantly increasing. Link-rate is the theoretical data transfer speed between your device and a gateway.</p>

<p><h1>4G/5G and LTE</h1></p>

<p>Long Term Evolution(LTE) is a 4G technology and the converged standard of GSM and CDMA. It is based on GSM and EDGE(Enhanced Data Rates for GSM Evolution). It supports download rates of 150 mbits/s, LTE Advanced(LTE-A) doubles that at 300 mbits/s.</p>

<p><h1>5G</h1></p>

<p>The fifth generation launched in 2020, operates at higher frequencies, hopefully achieving 10 gigabits per second with slower speeds being 100-900 mbits/s</p>

<p>This has changed the way for IoT devices, bandwidth is no longer a constraint, large data transfers are now available, faster monitoring and notificaitons, and more cloud processing.</p>

<p><h1>Satellite Networking</h1></p>

<p>Non-terrestrial communication, high cost relative to terrestrial networking. 100 mbits down, 5 mbits up are the most common. Remote sites / Difficult to network sites commonly use this form of communication.</p>

<p>Sattelites dish's have high latency 250ms up, 250ms down, if line of sight is not available neither is the network.</p>
