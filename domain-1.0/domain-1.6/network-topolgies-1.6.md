

<p><h1>What are Network Topolgies?</h1></p>

<p>The physical layout of a buildings network infrastructure.<br>
It also assists in understanding signal flow.</p>

<p><h1>Star Networks / Hub and Spoke</h1></p>

<p>Used in most large networks and all small networks, all devices connect to a central device.<br>
Switched ethernet is an example of a star network.</p>

<p><h1>Mesh Networks</h1></p>

<p>Multiple links to the same place either fully connected or partially connected.<br>
This type of network provides redundancy as if one of the connections fails, there is still a path.</p>

<P>Commonly used in WANs that maintains connectivity to a remote-site regardless of what conenction is available.</P>

![mesh-network](https://github.com/ch1c4g0/network-plus/blob/1548e6867c4ca28983033ce49f92e79f58b0a2dd/screenshots/Screenshot%202026-03-30%20at%2021-22-05%20mesh%20network%20-%20Google%20Search.png)

<p><h1>Hybrid Networks</h1></p>

<p>A combination of one or more physical topologies, most networks are hybrid.</p>

<p><h1>Spine and Leaf Architecture</h1></p>

<p>Each leaf switch connects to each spine, leaf switches do not connect to each other.<br>
This is very common for top-of-rack switching, each leaf is on the top of a physical network rack.<br>
This provides simple cabling, redundant connections, and fast.</p>
