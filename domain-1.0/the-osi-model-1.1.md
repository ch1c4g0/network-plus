<p><h1>What is the OSI model?</h1></p>

<p>OSI, or Open Systems Interconnect Model provides a broad overview of how<br>
data traverses our systems and infrastructure.</p>

<p>OSI has unique protocols at every layer.</p>

<p><h1>The 7 Layers</h1></p>

><p>OSI breaks down network communication into 7 layers,</p>
>
> - Physical(1)
> - Data Link(2)
> - Network(3)
> - Transport(4)
> - Session(5)
> - Presentation(6)
> - Application(7)

<p><h1>Breaking down each layer,</h1></p>

<p><h3>The Physical Layer</h3></p>

<p>These are the physical, tangible objects in networking.<br>
Signaling, Cabling, and Connectors are all examples of layer one items.</p>

<p>Network issues at Layer 1 will always deal with physical objects.</p>

<p><h3>The Data Link Layer</h3></p>

<p>This is commonly called the MAC Address Layer, it is the layer that is fundamental to<br>
two devices communicating over the network. It is also referred to as the switching layer.</p>

**<p>MAC Addresses are also called DLC or Data Link Control Protocols.</p>**

<p><h3>The Network Layer</h3></p>

<p>This is the layer routers use to determine how to route traffic. It is able to do this using<br>
IP Addresses to route traffic.</p>

**<p>Anything about routing can be chalked up to the Network Layer.</p>**

<p><h3>The Transport Layer</h3></p>

<p>This is often referred to as the post office layer, TCP and UDP operate here.<br>
It is responsible for transportation of traffic and packets over a network.</p>

<p><h3>The Session Layer</h3></p>

<p>Provides communicatiom management between point A and point B. The starting, stopping, and restarting<br>
is all done here.</p>

<p><h3>The Presentation Layer</h3></p>

**<p>What we will see</p>**

<p>In operation just before our eyes. Character Encoding, Application Encryption are all done here.<br>
The Presentation Layer and Application Layer are often merged.</p>

<p><h3>The Application Layer</h3></p>

**<p>What we see</p>**

<p>What we see and interact with, HTTP, DNS, FTP, and POP3 all interact here.</p>

><p><h1>Real World use of the OSI Model</h1></p>
>
> - Layer 1: Cables, Fibre Optic, and the signal itself.
> - Layer 2: Frame, MAC Addresses, EUI's (Extended Unique Identifier), Switches.
> - Layer 3: IP Addresses, Routers, Packets
> - Layer 4: TCP Segment, UDP Datagram
> - Layer 5: Control Protocols, tunneling protocols
> - Layer 6: Application Encryption (TLS/SSL)
> - Layer 7: Your eyeballs.
