

# Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEE)

A set of standards wireless standards that everyone follows ran by the 802.11 committee.

These are also referenced as generations

## Generations / Standards
- **802.11ac -- Wi-Fi 5**
- **802.11ax -- Wi-Fi 6 and Wi-Fi 6E (extended)**
- **802.11be -- Wi-Fi 7**

### 802.11 Technologies

#### Frequencies
- 2.4GHz
- 5 GHz
- 6 Ghz

#### Channels

Groups of frequencies numbered by the IEE

#### Bandwidth

The amount of frequency in use.

you'll commonly see.

- 20MHz
- 40MHz
- 80MHz
- 160MHz

**The higher the frequencies the more channels you have available to you, this also means you have more non-overlapping channels**

# Band Steering

Allows you to specify how users are able to use frequencies for the best possible connection. If band steering is not present, the strongest frequency is the one that is chosen. when band steering is turned on, the administrator gets to choose what frequency the user uses.

# 802.11h Standard

Prevents Wi-Fi networks from interfering with licensed operators like military radar, satelite systems, and Medical Devices **(WORLD-WIDE)**.

1. Dynamic Freqency Selection (DFS)
   Listens to wireless spectrum for licensed serivces, primarily radar. If the AP detects a primary user on its current channel, DFS forces the network to switch to a new non-overlapping channel to prevent data loss and signal disruption

2. Transmit Power Control (TPC)
   Automatically adjusts and reduces the radio (RF) frequency output power of both the AP and connected client devices, reducing RF pollution(interference) with neigboring networks. It also optimizes battery life for wireless clients.
